What led to Celebrology?

Terrorist attack in America -The September 11, 2001 Disaster:
  • Admin
  • July 7, 2022

Sola Odumosu grew up in a Nigerian society where many problems have been plaguing the nation’s history. He had voiced out in the way he could as a young man yet he knew his limitation. He made serious contributions to both local and national dailies as well as magazines.These were periods of the Nigerian nascent democracy, he discovered the menace of corruption in the nation and assumed intellectual radicalism to help solve the nation's problem. One day in the night , while contemplating on the Nigerian problems, there was a breaking news that terrorists had invaded United States of America. He watched the news on the television with panic and his worries about the world grew. He then shifted his attention to the world, thinking of what to do, to help solve global problems. Sola Odumosu developed a very high taste for intellectual radicalism and thought of inventing a new field of knowledge to solve world problems.

He first thought within himself about "human dignity", and in the mean time discovered that the reason there is problem in the world is because there is lack of respect for human dignity. He then thought of inventing a field of knowledge that will concentrate on human dignity; the idea of Celebrology had begun ! But it took Sola Odumosu some years to coin Celebrology. One day around 2003, he found himself facing the mirror and was talking to himself. Suddenly, a question struck in his mind that supposing people came around and asked him what he was doing, what would he say? He then answered himself this way " I think I am looking for the scientific study of honour that places value on what I am looking for".

Sola Odumosu was always found with this idea anytime he wants to plumb the depth of his being; the phrase " scientific study of honour " struck his mind. He carefully reasoned with the fundamental truth that honour can only find expression in human dignity, but he was conscious of the name he would give to the new knowledge and he began to search for the languages he would use. He thought about Greek and Latin; the reason he did this was to honour Europe and appreciate their pioneering role in knowledge formation, appreciating Greek and Latin as the intellectual languages of Europe over the years. He then looked for the Latin word for honour which he found in "celebrare” and "logos" the Greek word for scientific study. Now celebrare and logos were combined together and first pronounced celebrarelogos and better pronounced celebralogy and best pronounced CELEBROLOGY. Celebrology therefore literally means the scientific study of honour.

However, it must be told that apart from Latin and Greek, an African language known as Yoruba remains the bedrock of the intellectual wizardry of Celebrology. Most words have been coined by Sola Odumosu from Yoruba into Celebrology for example, a word such as fefetule was coined from the Yoruba words fi efe tule which literally mean to charge the ground with humour.Theoretically, it has been proposed by Sola Odumosu as the basis of carnival all over the world. Sola Odumosu, while creating the theoretical base of Celebrology developed what he calls superwaves on which he developed the concept of (i) universalism, that human dignity is universal and it is the possession of mankind and wherever humans exist, they have dignity that must be respected; (ii) constant-dynamism, that human dignity is constant everywhere and their behaviour is different; (iii) generation-persistism, that human dignity persists in every generation irrespective of the age humans have ever lived and will still live on the planet earth.

Furthering his discoveries, Sola embarked on publications to project his ideas to the world. Soon or later, he began to gain popularity for his idea. Meanwhile, as at the 10th of June 2008, he had registered Celebrology International in Nigeria, to project his discoveries on Celebrology. In 2009, he published his official journal FATHER OF CELEBROLOGY ACOMOID with the headline: Study Ojude Oba in Celebrology. Not too many people as at then were willing to support his idea because they considered it too complicated. He took pains to explain his findings to the people. Sola targeted the Ijebus' aristocrats and greatly persuaded them to support his idea. His first visit to Otunba Olasubomi Balogun, founder of FCMB was not favorable. Disappointed, yet he summoned courage to move on, believing in himself. Great Ijebus who later extended their support towards projecting Celebrology were: Chief Bayo Kuku, the Ogbeni Oja of Ijebuland; Asiwaju Olajide Awosedo, founder of Grant Properties; Dr Sonny Kuku, Olor'ogun of Ijebuland, co-founder of EKO Hospital; Otunba Fatai Arowolo, co-founder ARON Construction Company to mention but few.
