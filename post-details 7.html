SENATOR IBIKUNLE AMOSUN TO BE HONOURED WITH WORLD CELEBRATIONS AMBASSADOR''S AWARD after SALLAH
- First to receive the award in Nigeria and Africa. Released to the Nigerian Media on the 7th of July, 2022. by Enganimn Sola Odumosu Father of Celebrology and the World Leading Celebration Thinker

Ladies and Gentlemen of the Press,

1. THE MAN TO BE GIVEN AN AWARD

I am obliged to make it known to all and sundry on behalf of Celebrology International, a Nigerian global think-tank that is dedicated to the intellectual pursuit of human dignity that the former Governor of Ogun State and the currently serving Senator of the Federal Republic of Nigeria, Senator Ibikunle Amosun is being nominated to receive an international award from Celebrology International as the World Celebrations Ambassador, which is one of the highest honours being given to an individual by the global think-tank, and Amosun remains the first in Nigeria to bag this award. This is as a result of his commitment and creative audacity in promoting global celebrations. As a global authority on celebration and indeed the World Leading Celebration Thinker, I led the postulation of the celebration theory in the book WHAT IS CELEBRATION?

WHAT IS CELEBRATION? - Leading the World through the Tunnel of My Thinking Revolution.,
A book that is popularly known as the book of celebration theory, where I present the scope of global celebrations with explicit reverence to their continental base. While I critically engage the mind on the "stage" of performance, I make it clear that distance and proximity does not in anyway affect the admiration or affection that people have in places for celebration irrespective of where they originate from. Such is the essence of celebration acceptability itself that means the humanitarian embrace of celebration in places irrespective of geographical location.

2. THE SCOPE

In capturing the scope of global celebrations, two sets of such celebrations are itemised and brought to limelight. These are INDIGENOUS CELEBRATIONS and NON-INDIGENOUS CELEBRATIONS. Indigenous celebrations are the celebrations which originate from a given nation or society and which is capable of proliferating to other parts of the world while the non-indigenous celebrations are the celebrations which originate from nations or societies which are borrowed into other nations or socities. The overwhelming persistence and the recurrence of such celebrations in the course of time in which such celebrations later become the celebrations of these people is called LOANCLAIMANCE (in Celebrology).

3. THE QUALIFICATION CRITERIA

- WHAT QUALIFIES AMOSUN FOR THE AWARD However, for someone to be qualified as WORLD CELEBRATIONS AMBASSADOR, he must have positioned himself in promoting both the indigenous and non-indigenous celebrations that make up global celebrations in the nation he resides either by using his creative skills or official position in a public office he or she occupies. While celebration itself exists in four kinds in form of festival, carnival, actival and party; it is pertinent to make it known that there are nine classes to which celebration is grouped: (i) irepedic celebration
(ii) patriotic celebration
(iii) religious celebration
(iv) cultural celebration
(v) life celebration
(vi) love celebration (vii)success celebration
(viii) professional celebration and
(ix) new year celebration. Now both indigenous and non-indigenous celebrations find their base in the above classes of celebration on which an individual must have demonstrated explicit prowess in fostering:
(1) International Fraternity
(2) National Integration

4. THE INDIGENOUS CELEBRATIONS THAT AMOSUN PROMOTED

(i) Ojude Oba, Lisabi and Oronna Festivals Nigeria and indeed Africa as a whole is noted for cultural celebrations which are really indigenous to the people as a result of their agelong tradition. In Ogun State in particular where Senator Ibikunle Amosun was a Governor for 8 years, he promoted the following indigenous cultural celebrations of the people of Ogun State:
(a) Ojude Oba in Ijebu (Ijebu-Ode)
(b) Lisabi in Egba (Abeokuta)
(c) Oronna in Yewa (Ilaro)
All these he brought them to global limelight by giving them all the support needed during his tenure as Governor of Ogun State.
(ii) African Drums Festival
Perhaps the most notable and glaring for which Senator Ibikunle Amosun is famous in Africa and the world at large, African Drums Festival was invented by the Ibikunle Amosun-led administration and brought to global limelight. Four editions of it were staged with beautiful performances of people who came from nations of Africa and beyond. While this fully encouraged international fraternity, the essence of the message was not lost. The celebration to a greater extent, boosted business in the capital city of the State as well as those involved. African Drums Festival is the first of its kind and ever since, none has ever been like it.
(iii) Ogun State @ 40
When Ogun State clocked 40 in 2016, the then Governor of Ogun State, Senator Ibikunle Amosun used what is called FIBIKARON (a celebration phenomenon that refers to the process of using the number of years of a birthday in proportion to the numbers of projects being executed at the moment) in Celebrology, to add glamour to the celebration, unveiled 40 laudable projects to bring development to the State. These were the first of its kind and it really sent the signal of inspiration to other States across the country.
(iv) The Special Superlative Birthdays
Special Superlative Birthdays are usually indulged in by the living legends who occupy certain official positions in the society in which they use their birthdays to add value to lives. The then Governor of Ogun State, Senator Ibikunle Amosun demonstrated this, executed projects, set the prisoners free, gave charity to the needies, brought joy to many.

5. THE NON-INDIGENOUS CELEBRATIONS THAT AMOSUN ALSO PROMOTED
Senator Ibikunle Amosun explored a wide range of celebrations in their explicit classes. The United Nations designated days (irepedic celebrations) were those he really promoted such as World Environment Day, International Day of the Girl Child, World Water Day among others to enhance human dignity.
Senator Amosun gave the widely acclaimed religious celebrations such as Christmas, Eid-al-Adha and New Year a meaning among Christians and Muslims and willingly graced celebrations in churches and mosques, promoted religious tolerance in Ogun State at large.

6. DATE AND THE VENUE
The award shall be presented on Sunday 17th of July, 2022 to him in his mansion at Abeokuta or any other designated place the legislator feels convenient and appropriate to receive the prestigious award.

In conclusion, the celebration-loving personality, Senator Ibikunle Amosun deserves this prestigious award which is the first of its kind, since three years he left office as Governor of Ogun State for the Red-Chamber in Abuja. He is an inspiration to all and an encouragement to our celebration think-tank and we believe, will take up greater responsibilities in promoting the celebration cause in the National House of Assembly as a Senator of the Federal Republic of Nigeria, supporting initiatives that aim at promoting international fraternity and national unity through celebrations.
