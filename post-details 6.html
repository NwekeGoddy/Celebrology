THE RETURN OF OJUDE OBA IN 2022

Sanwo-Olu Graces Celebration as Ijebus Defy Rain.
  • Admin
  • July 14, 2022


Barely two weeks to the Ijebu's biggest celebration, a press conference surfaced to create a media awareness of the re-emergence of the recurring celebration that was interrupted by the pandemic, causing a "face-off" for 2 years, thus the theme: The Return of Ojude Oba.
The much anticipated celebration which is usually held on the third day of Eid-el- Kabir took place on Monday 11th of July, 2022 amidst a heavy downpour. However, despite the rain, Ijebus proceeded with their celebration, with horseriders making equestrian prowess on their horses and different age-grades parading in their expensive attires.
The 2022 edition of Ojude-Oba festival in Ijebu-Ode, sponsored by Globacom with contract being renewed for another ten years, reached its peak with presentation of prizes to winners of various competitions.
Dazzling in excitement, Globacom took its corporate celebration outdoor ( marodey) to the Ijebus presented tricycles, popularly called “Keke”, to five lucky subscribers.
Also, 10 Glo customers went home with television sets, while 10 others were presented grinding machines.

The Ijebu age-grade system popularly known as "Regberegbe" took it toll as different age grades stormed the venue to steal the show, competing in attires and pageantry. Since Globacom took over the sponsorship, winners in various categories have been rewarded with prizes. In this year's competition, Egbe Obayori Okunrin Asiwaju emerged the best-dressed age grade and won N500,000, while Egbe Tobalase Okunrin and Egbe Bobamayegun won N300,000 and N200,000 respectively as first and second runners-up in the male category
The Egbe Bobamayegun Obinrin Asiwaju emerged the winner with Egbe Arobayo Obinrin and Egbe Tobalase Obinrin taking toll as runners-up in the female category, won the same prizes like their male counterparts.

The horseriders displayed their elegance in the mock battle show. This wouldn't go without a contest, Balogun Kuku dynasty won the admiration of all, its skills and dexterity on their horses appealed to the imagination coming first, while Balogun Odunuga and Balogun Gamugasa came second and third, winning N500,000, N300,000 and N200,000 respectively.

In a detribalised beauty peageant, 19-year-old Oluwafunmilade Osotekun, a student of Federal University of Agriculture, Abeokuta, emerged Miss Ojude-Oba 2022. She received N150,000 cash prize and Glo gift items. Christiana Adenike Ogunwusi, 23, of Ogun State College of Health Technology, Ilese-Ijebu, came second to win N100,000, while 23-year-old beautician, Ogunsanwo Nafisat Abisola, came third, winning N50,000.

Awujale of Ijebuland, Oba Sikiru Kayode Adetona, in an address on his behalf by the Cordinator, Ojude Oba Planning Committee, Dr. Fassy Yusuf , applauded Globacom for its consistent support for the festival since 2006. Other co-sponsors are FCMB, Rites Food, including Nigerian

This year's celebration was graced by the Governor of Lagos State, Babajide Sanwo-Olu as the Special Guest of Honour. He was hosted by Governor Dapo Abiodun of Ogun State. Governor Sanwo-Olu drummed support for Asiwaju Tinubu.

"Distinguished guests, ladies and gentlemen, let me use this opportunity to make this rallying call on all true sons and daughters of the Yoruba race and pronounce that in the unifying spirit of the Ojude Oba celebration, it is my firm hope and belief that the Yoruba race will unite as one behind the momentous candidature of Asiwaju Bola Ahmed Tinubu, a proud son of the race and one of the most formidable and experienced politicians to have ever emerged from Africa. Yoruba Lo’ kan! Asiwaju Lo’ kan!,” he said.

Sanwo-Olu was the third governor of Lagos State to participate in the annual festival which was last held in 2019. The celebration was interrupted for two years, 2020 and 2021 by the COVD-19 pandemic. Former Governors of Lagos State who once participated in Ojude Oba were: Sir Micheal Otedola and Asiwaju Ahmed Bola Tinubu..

The celebration that has been tagged Africa's No 1 Biggest Cutural Celebration and published in The Book of World Celebrations (2019 and 2021 editions) being published by Celebrology International, hosted by the Awujale of Ijebu and Paramount Ruler of Ijebu Land, Oba Sikiru Kayode Adetona was attended by dignitaries including the State Governor and his deputy, Mr Dapo Abiodun, Noimot Salako-Oyedele, past governors of the state Aremo Olusegun Osoba, and Otunba Gbenga Daniel. Other dignitaries at the occasion were Otunba (Professor)Toyin Ashiru, Otunba Bimbo Ashiru, Alhaji Adebola Sulaiman Adegunwa, Otunba Yomi Odunowo and many others.
