The Postulation of Celebration Theory:

  • Admin
  • July 7, 2022

Sola Odumosu propounded the celebration theory in his intellectual reaction to Abuja 2010 terrorist bomblast of October 1. It was the day Nigeria was celebrating its 50th independence Day during President Jonathan's regime. Some highly aggrieved Nigerians led by Charles Okah on October 1, 2010 invaded Eagle Square and its surroundings to carry out one of early attempted threat and violence on celebration in the nation's history. This was widely condemned by many Nigerians and the international community who saw it as not only a crime against Nigerians but against humanity, for devalueing and deforming celebration which remains the common heritage of mankind. Okah's criminal action later fueled so many terrorist atrocities of the Boko Haram, resulting in UN Building bombings and other government offices were largely targeted most especially in Abuja and its environs. The Christmas Day 2011 terrorist attacks at various quarters in the country sent much worry, awakening greater celebrological concern. Sola Odumosu released his celebration manifestoes entitled: The Universal Declaration of Celebration Principles (UDCPs) which was launched on the 30th of December, 2010 at Daniel Akintonde Kids Arcade Multipurpose Hall, Italapo/Oyingbo, Ijebu-Ode. The event was chaired by the University of Lagos Vice Chancellor, Professor Adetokunbo Sofoluwe.

This further led to an enduring relationship between Sola Odumosu and University of Lagos in expanding the frontiers of knowledge of Celebrology. Following the online information on the discovery, in January 2011, Sisira Neti, an Indian scholar and researcher was the first to reference Sola Odumosu's work in an international journal, published in Asia, International Journal of Research in Management, Science and Technology, where she wrote on 'corporate celebrations'. Odumosu's universal definition of celebration became a template of reasoning. Dalphine Scott from US, on his Super Fantastic Leadership Show found this same definition ideal and cited it in his work. Series of global writers aknowledged Sola Odumosu's effort. Although, immortalism, a branch of study of Celebrology achieved greater enlightenment on UNILAG FM 103.1, Sola Odumosu's immortalism theory exerted much influence especially in the immortalism of the late UNILAG VC, Professor Adetokunbo Sofoluwe who died in office in 2012, a park was named after him in front of the Senate Building.

However, more attention was focused on the celebration theory on UNILAG FM 103.1 in the program 'ScienceWorld'. Meanwhile on October 1, 2011, it is pertinent to flash back, Sola Odumosu was hosted on Eko FM 89.75 on Lagos This Morning in a program anchored by Funmi Omoboriowo. Two other personalities were hosted along with him: a Nigerian elder statesman, Pa Hassan Sumonu and President of Nigeria Youth Council, Sotade George. While both were to speak from the elder statesman and youth perspectives, Sola Odumosu was to address the issue from the celebrological perspective, as he was the first to open the floor with the question, 'what is celebration?'. He applied the celebration theory, which he propounded to independence day celebration. Phones came in from few Nigerians. The program was aired to the nation, reaching over 5 million listeners. Sola Odumosu has been featured in several other media both print and electronic till date.

In 2016, the book 'What Is Celebration?' was published. This remains his major work on the celebration theory. In 2019, Sola Odumosu published his findings on the Nigerian Living Legends in his official journal, Father of Celebrology Acomoid. This's a national project he embarked upon. Using Father of Celebrology Acomoid to enlighten the people on his discovery on birthday celebration, especially the superlative birthday, during the 80th birthday celebration of a highly distinguished Nigerian Librarian, Asiwaju Gbolade Osunkoya, held in Odosenbora, he published a work introducing 'special superlative birthday'. This was to complement his earlier work on biography having authored a book to celebrate an elder states woman, Chief Mrs Omolara Kusanu which was published on her 80th birthday in January 2015.

In August 2019, Sola Odumosu released the maiden edition of The Book of World Celebrations where he ranked 10 global cultural celebrations. This was published to mark Ojude Oba 2019, the Ijebu biggest celebration, which has been ranked No. 1 in Africa and 4th in the world in the class of cultural celebrations. To complement this, he began publishing World Celebrations Magazine in 2019.

Sola Odumosu dives into the practicality of his theories, hence its relevance to the academics and the larger society as a whole, consulting for the academics, apart from his scholarly lecture, visitation and publication, The Father of Celebrology who is universally known as the World Leading Celebration Thinker currently runs a blog on celebration, which has also made him the No.1 celebration blogger in the world, attracting millions of visitors everyday.
