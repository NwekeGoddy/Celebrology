WHAT IS CELEBROLOGY?
  • Coined by Sola Odumosu, the Nigerian-born Intellectual Genius who is the Father of Celebrology, from a Latin word celebrare which means honour and a Greek word logos which means scientific study, Celebrology literally means the scientific study of honour.
  • Editor
  • October 11, 2022



The intellectual development of Celebrology comes with diverse theoretical postulations of its founding Father from the native intelligence of African creativity, Sola Odumosu, who remains the universal authority of the knowledge, having created its schools of thought and branches of study. Celebrology is based on the pragmatic theories of the evidential experience in our world throughout ages on human dignity in the thinking radicalization of human dignity as expression for honour in:

      1. Universalism

      2. Constantism and Dynamism

      3. Generation-persistism.



The theoretical postulations of Celebrology revolve around the above which are otherwise known as the superwaves.

TRANSFIGURISM, To understand Celebrology is to dive into transfigurism, which is often described as the entrance door into the intellectual house of Celebrology. Honour finds expression in human dignity, thus to gain insight into honour is to have an understanding of human dignity, for there is no honour without dignity. To have honour is to have dignity, live with dignity and respect this same dignity in others.This is called transfigurism.

THE SCHOOLS OF THOUGHT Celebrology is divided into two schools of thought.
These are:
    1.Templatism and
    2. Apexiom


Templatism maintains the ground that honour is not by position, hence it is dignity of character that counts. When one lives a life of dignity, he automatically earns honour from members of the society which in turn makes him a hero or legend in their midst. Templatism is formed in
    (i) Potency; that is, the potential and skill an individual possesses in earning honour for himself, and
    (ii) Activism; that is, the revolutionary struggle for freedom.


However, there are cases where an individual who is templatism-driven aspires for the position of honour in the society. Such is known as ladderation. This has been the basis of every revolutionary patriotism that has birthed nations and re-sustains patriotic legacies. The templatists are always in argument with the apexiomists and have used ladderation to drive home their points in practical instances, citing the case of George Washington in America and Nelson Mandela in South Africa.

Apexiom hangs on position as the means through which honour can be earned. It is devised in:
    (1) Power; the monachical power and political power and
    (2) Wealth by means of aristocratic exploits and discovery might.


Perhaps the area of controversy in Celebrology, apexiom hands over to us the notion of position effects as the attainment of honour. Power and wealth the way they are played in Nigeria and in other African countries have formed the subject matter of apexiom.

THE BRANCHES OF CELEBROLOGY There are six branches of Celebrology:
    (1) Konolaric
    (2) Majimrar
    (3) Immortalism
    (4) Keroepaporh
    (5) Celebration
    (6) Conferism
